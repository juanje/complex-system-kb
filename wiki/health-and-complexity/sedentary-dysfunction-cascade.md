# Sedentary Dysfunction Cascade

Modern sedentary life creates a systematic chain of physical dysfunctions where each problem exacerbates the next, forming an interconnected cascade from feet to neck rather than a collection of isolated symptoms. The chain follows the joint-by-joint principle: dysfunction at one joint forces compensations in its neighbors, which create new dysfunctions, which force further compensations. Chronic pains -- back, neck, shoulder, knee, hip -- are emergent outcomes of this cascade, not root causes.

## Key points

- **The cascade runs bottom-up through the body.** Weak feet → rigid ankles → unstable knees → rigid/weak hips → unstable trunk → rigid thoracic spine → uncontrolled scapulae → rigid shoulders → tense neck. Each link creates the conditions for the next dysfunction.
- **Dysfunction propagates through the joint-by-joint principle.** When a mobility joint becomes rigid, its neighboring stability joint is forced into compensatory mobility. When a stability joint becomes unstable, its neighboring mobility joint locks down. The alternating pattern means a single dysfunction can cascade across the entire chain.
- **Chronic pains are emergent outcomes, not root causes.** Knee pain is typically an emergent symptom of ankle rigidity and/or hip dysfunction -- the knee is caught between two failing neighbors. Treating the knee as the problem addresses a symptom while the cascade continues.
- **The three steps intervention targets highest-leverage points.** Rather than treating individual symptoms, the framework identifies where in the cascade intervention produces the greatest systemic effect -- addressing root constraints rather than emergent symptoms.
- **Environmental catalog of sedentary constraints.** Each element of the modern environment continuously imposes specific restrictions: flat floors → ankle dorsiflexion restriction; chairs → hip flexion restriction and glute inhibition; shoes → foot weakness and proprioceptive loss; screens → eye strain and forward head posture; chest-height objects → shoulder elevation restriction; soft food → jaw and facial musculature restriction.
- **Each constraint operates continuously and unconsciously.** These are not occasional exposures but permanent environmental defaults. A person spends 8+ hours sitting, 16+ hours in shoes, and all waking hours on flat surfaces. Willpower alone cannot counteract forces that operate every minute of every day -- the intervention must be environmental.

## Examples

- **Ankle rigidity causing "knee problems."** A person develops knee pain descending stairs. Examination reveals severely restricted ankle dorsiflexion from years of flat surfaces and supportive shoes. The ankle cannot bend enough during descent, so the knee absorbs compensatory forces at angles it is not designed for. Restoring ankle mobility resolves the knee pain -- the "knee problem" was an emergent symptom of an ankle constraint.
- **Lumbar spine compensating for rigid hips.** A person with chronic lower back pain shows restricted hip flexion from years of sitting. Every time they bend forward, the lumbar spine (which should remain stable) flexes to compensate for the hips that will not. The lumbar pain is an emergent outcome of hip restriction.
- **Shoes on flat surfaces creating ankle rigidity.** Modern shoes with elevated heels and rigid soles eliminate the ankle's need to dorsiflex, evert, or adapt to terrain. Combined with exclusively flat surfaces, the ankle receives no stimulus to maintain its mobility. Over years, the nervous system restricts the range it considers unnecessary -- and the cascade begins.

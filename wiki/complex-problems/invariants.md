# Invariants

Invariants are properties or relationships within a complex system that remain constant regardless of the system's dynamic behavior. While complex systems are non-linear and unpredictable, some properties always hold: gravity affects objects on Earth, water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius, objects less dense than water float. These constant properties provide predictable anchors within an otherwise unpredictable system, making them extremely useful for decision-making even when the system's dynamic behavior cannot be predicted.

## Key points

- Invariants can be observed or deduced and serve as reliable constraints for decision-making. They are the bedrock on which skills and strategies can be built, because they are the aspects of the system you can count on regardless of how the situation evolves.
- Identifying invariants is part of the skill development process. The more experience you have with a type of problem, the better you become at detecting which properties are invariant and which are dynamic. This is one of the key differences between experts and novices: experts know the invariants, which dramatically simplifies their reading of the situation.
- In the fitness landscape metaphor, invariants correspond to features of the terrain that do not move even when the peaks are dancing. Attractors may shift, but invariants hold. This is why multidisciplinary experts in a complex domain are valuable -- each knows different invariants from their field.
- Not everything that appears stable is invariant. Some properties hold for long periods but eventually change (quasi-invariants). Distinguishing true invariants from temporary regularities is itself a skill that develops through experience and contrast.

## Examples

- Conference invariants: there must be a program with enough speakers for all sessions; roles must be defined (organizers, speakers, attendees, helpers); a budget must exist covering venue, materials, publicity, and transport; funding is needed (sponsorships, tickets, donations); and there will always be unexpected events requiring adjustments. These hold regardless of the specific conference.
- Physical invariants: gravity, freezing point of water, buoyancy rules -- these do not change regardless of the dynamic behavior of the complex systems operating within their domain.
- In the context of learning about a specific complex system, invariants are one of three observable categories alongside behaviors (attractors, emergent behaviors) and structures (networks, interconnections). They provide the stable reference frame against which dynamic changes can be interpreted.

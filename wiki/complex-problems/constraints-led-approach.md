# Constraints-Led Approach

The Constraints-Led Approach (CLA) is a sports pedagogy methodology built on the principle that the coach does not teach the correct technique but designs the practice environment through constraints (field size, net height, rules, time) that force the learner to find solutions within a bounded space. A direct practical application of Bernstein's freeze-release principle, applied deliberately by an external agent rather than emerging naturally as in infant development.

## Key points

- **Three types of constraints.** The coach manipulates task constraints (rules, goals, time), environmental constraints (field size, equipment), and performer constraints (body limitations) to shape the search space.
- **Constraints define terrain, not solutions.** Constraints do not dictate the solution -- they define the terrain where solutions can be found. This produces more adaptive solutions because the learner self-organizes within the constraint landscape rather than memorizing prescribed movements.
- **Independent rediscovery across fields.** The principle that effective learning requires well-designed constraints has been independently rediscovered by at least four fields: sports pedagogy (CLA), developmental psychology (children born with deliberately closed dimensions), software engineering (scoped onboarding), and artificial intelligence (2026 Dupoux-LeCun-Malik paper citing Bernstein directly).
- **AI agents and bounded instructions.** In AI: agents perform better with constrained instructions (skills: specific patterns, tools, and order for specific task types) than with full context -- the AI field is rediscovering Bernstein's principle that systems that learn or execute need well-designed constraints.
- "When four disciplines that do not talk to each other arrive at the same place, there is usually something real underneath."

## Examples

- **Coaching through constraints.** A good coach does not say "do exactly this movement." They say "the objective is to touch the ball before it crosses this line, and you can only use this side of your body." The constraint defines the terrain; the learner finds the how.
- **Children as constrained systems.** Children are not reduced adults -- they are systems born with deliberately closed dimensions (initial myopia, limited attention, restricted motor range). Each limitation functions as a curriculum that prioritizes the learnable over the possible.
- **AI code agents.** "The more open the space, the worse they perform. They get distracted, lose focus, make errors they would not make with more bounded instructions."

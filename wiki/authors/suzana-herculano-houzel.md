# Suzana Herculano-Houzel

Brazilian neuroscientist known for redefining how we count neurons and for reframing intelligence as behavioral flexibility (repertoire) rather than computational capacity. Her broadest definition of behavior -- any observable action driven by energy flow -- clarifies that what makes animal behavior special is not behavior itself but the flexibility the nervous system adds to it.

## Key contributions

- Developed the isotropic fractionator method for accurately counting neurons, overturning the long-held "100 billion neurons" estimate
- Redefined intelligence operationally as the capacity to vary actions adaptively -- a dog ignoring or chasing the same ball on different occasions demonstrates intelligence through repertoire, while a plant always growing toward light does not
- Broadest possible definition of behavior: any observable action driven by energy flow (includes moons orbiting, fans spinning). The nervous system adds flexibility -- the ability to not do the same thing every time

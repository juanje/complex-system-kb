# Rafe Kelley

Movement practitioner and founder of Evolve Move Play. His work centers on using play-fighting and movement games to develop both physical and social skills simultaneously. He demonstrates progressive play-fighting games that teach movement-based communication and opponent-reading, gradually increasing intensity from cooperative to competitive contexts. His approach treats play as a complexity-native learning tool that develops calibration and adaptive capacity.

## Key contributions

- **Progressive play-fighting methodology.** Designs sequences of games that start cooperative and gradually increase in competitive intensity, allowing participants to develop movement-based communication, spatial awareness, and opponent-reading skills in a safe, self-regulating context.
- **Play as movement education.** Demonstrates that play-fighting and movement games are not recreational add-ons but primary vehicles for developing physical literacy, social attunement, and adaptive movement capacity -- skills that formal training environments struggle to produce.
- **Bridging movement and social skill development.** His games require participants to read and respond to another person's intentions through movement, developing perception-action coupling in an interpersonal context.

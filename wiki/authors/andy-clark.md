# Andy Clark

Philosopher of mind and cognitive scientist who argues in "The Experience Machine" that the brain is fundamentally a prediction machine -- continuously predicting sensory input and correcting errors. This predictive processing framework explains why linear-deterministic thinking is the human cognitive default: our brains evolved to detect cause-and-effect patterns and make small predictions about what will happen next. The mechanism works well for immediate, low-complexity interactions but systematically fails when applied to complex systems.

## Key contributions

- Developed the predictive processing framework: the brain continuously generates predictions about sensory input and updates when those predictions fail, creating a cycle of prediction and correction that shapes all perception and cognition
- His work explains the cognitive basis of linear-deterministic thinking -- our prediction machinery evolved for low-complexity environments and creates systematic errors when applied to non-linear, interdependent systems
- Author of "The Experience Machine" (recommended reading in the blog), which provides theoretical grounding for understanding why humans default to proportional, turn-based reasoning
- Also known for the extended mind thesis (with David Chalmers), arguing that cognitive processes extend beyond the brain into tools and environment -- directly relevant to embodied and extended cognition

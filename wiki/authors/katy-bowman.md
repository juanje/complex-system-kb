# Katy Bowman

Movement scientist, biomechanics expert, and author of "Move Your DNA." Founded Nutritious Movement. Her central contribution is the framework treating movement diversity as nutritional diversity for cellular health -- reframing sedentarism as movement malnutrition rather than simple inactivity. Advocates for environment design as a practical tool: she removed all conventional seating from her home to make movement variety the default rather than a willpower-dependent choice.

## Key contributions

- **Movement-as-nutrition framework.** Coined and developed the analogy mapping movement types to macronutrients and micronutrients, making the case that cells require diverse mechanical inputs the same way they require diverse nutritional inputs. This framework makes movement variety a tractable, assessable variable rather than an abstract health recommendation.
- **Sedentarism as malnutrition, not inactivity.** Reframes the problem from "not enough exercise" to "not enough movement variety." A marathon runner who only runs is movement-malnourished despite high activity volume -- the same way someone who eats only rice is nutritionally malnourished despite adequate calories.
- **Environment design as practice.** Demonstrates that environment modification (removing furniture, using floor-level seating) is more reliable than willpower for changing movement patterns, providing a concrete example of affordance-based intervention.

# Nassim Taleb

Risk analyst and essayist who created the concepts of "Black Swan" events and "Antifragility." His work on how highly impactful, unforeseen events arise from non-linear dynamics in complex systems, and on how certain systems improve from perturbation rather than merely surviving it, is referenced across multiple articles in the blog.

## Key contributions
- Coined the "Black Swan" concept: rare, high-impact, unforeseen events that are products of non-linear properties within complex systems such as the economy, climate, and sociopolitical contexts
- Developed "Antifragility" as a category distinct from resilience -- antifragile systems gain from disorder, whereas resilient systems merely return to a stable state after perturbation
- Provided frameworks for thinking about risk, fragility, and robustness that connect directly to attractor theory and phase transitions in complex systems

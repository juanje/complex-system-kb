# Edward Lorenz

Meteorologist who discovered sensitivity to initial conditions while working on weather prediction models and coined the famous "butterfly effect" metaphor: the flapping of a butterfly's wings in Brazil could theoretically cause a tornado in Texas. His work established a foundational concept in chaos theory and complexity science -- that deterministic systems can produce unpredictable behavior because tiny differences in initial measurements cascade through non-linear interactions into wildly different outcomes.

## Key contributions

- Discovered sensitivity to initial conditions through weather prediction research: small rounding differences in initial measurements produced completely different forecast trajectories
- Coined the "butterfly effect" metaphor, one of the most widely recognized concepts in complexity science
- His work provided the empirical and theoretical foundation for understanding why complex systems are inherently unpredictable beyond short time horizons, despite being technically deterministic

# Eliyahu M. Goldratt

Physicist and management consultant who developed the Theory of Constraints, which views system performance as limited by its bottlenecks -- improving non-bottleneck components does not improve overall system performance. His novel "The Goal" ("La meta") is referenced in the blog as essential reading for understanding how bottlenecks arise, affect, and can be managed within complex systems, and for demonstrating why local optimization of subsystems destroys global performance.

## Key contributions

- Developed the Theory of Constraints: a system's throughput is determined by its most constrained component, and improving anything else is waste
- Authored "The Goal" (1984), a novel about operations management that makes systems thinking accessible through narrative -- recommended in the blog alongside Scott E. Page and Nassim Taleb
- His bottleneck analysis provides one of the clearest demonstrations of why local optimization is counterproductive in interconnected systems

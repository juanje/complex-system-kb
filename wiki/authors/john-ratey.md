# John Ratey

Clinical professor of psychiatry at Harvard Medical School, known for his research on the neuroscience of exercise and its effects on brain function. His work demonstrates that physical activity drives neuroplasticity through mechanisms like BDNF production.

## Key contributions

- "Spark: The Revolutionary New Science of Exercise and the Brain" — establishes the neurological pathways through which exercise improves cognitive function, learning capacity, and mental health
- BDNF research — documents how movement generates brain-derived neurotrophic factor, promoting creation of new neurons and neural connections
- Neural reuse — contributes to understanding that neurons created through physical activity can be repurposed for cognitive tasks, providing a direct pathway from physical training to learning capacity

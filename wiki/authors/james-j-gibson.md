# James J. Gibson

American psychologist who founded ecological psychology. Key insight: perception is not the brain constructing an internal model of the world but the direct detection of information in the environment. Introduced the concept of affordances -- relational properties of the organism-environment system, not of objects or agents alone.

## Key contributions

- Founded ecological psychology: the study of organism-environment as an indivisible unit, rejecting the separation of perception from action
- Coined "affordances" -- action possibilities that exist in the relationship between an organism and its environment, not in either alone. A step affords climbing to someone who can flex their knees, not to a wheelchair user.
- Established perception-action coupling: perception and action are inseparable -- what we perceive depends on what we can do, and what we can do depends on what we perceive

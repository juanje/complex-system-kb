# Arturo Goicochea

Neurologist and author specializing in pain as a brain-generated perception. His work challenges the conventional view that chronic pain reflects ongoing tissue damage, arguing instead that the brain produces pain based on learned threat evaluations that can persist long after tissue healing.

## Key contributions

- "Migraña: Una pesadilla cerebral" (Migraine: A Brain Nightmare) — frames migraine and chronic pain as brain-generated perceptions driven by learned patterns, not structural pathology
- Pain education as therapy — demonstrates that understanding pain mechanisms measurably reduces pain intensity and improves function
- Bridges clinical neurology with complexity-compatible thinking about the nervous system's protective, self-organizing nature
